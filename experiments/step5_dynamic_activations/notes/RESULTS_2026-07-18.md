# Dynamic activation results — 2026-07-18

## Hypothesis

Start training with `ReLU(x)^2`, gradually increase the use of
`GELU(x)^2`, and finish with GELU². The intended trade-off is early
exploration, late stability, and low inference cost.

## Implementations

- `SquaredReLU` and `SquaredGELU`.
- Weighted schedule: computes both activations and linearly interpolates them.
- Stochastic hard schedule: samples one branch per optimizer step and hot-swaps
  every FFN to the native chosen activation. There is no conditional branch or
  double activation computation in the model forward.

The hot-swap controller caches the target FFNs. Its measured CPU overhead fell
from approximately 0.49 ms to 0.067 ms per optimizer step.

## Benchmark correction

An initial `variable_tracking` run was invalid: `long_context_accuracy.py` is
evaluation-only, so `--steps 500` evaluated a randomly initialized model for
500 batches. The CLI and output now state this explicitly, and task-generator
and oracle-metric tests prevent the same interpretation error.

The trained pipeline was calibrated as follows:

| Sequence length | Training | Variable-tracking accuracy |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 64 | 500 steps, 3 seeds | 98.83–99.61% |
| 256 | 500 steps, 3 seeds | 74.22–98.83%, mean 89.97% |

This established that the task is learnable and that length 256 exposes
optimization variance.

## Trained comparison

Configuration: `engram_noconv`, symbolic input, sequence length 256, batch 4,
500 training steps, 64 evaluation batches, seeds 0/1/2. The model trains on
variable tracking and is also evaluated out-of-task on passkey and multi-query
retrieval.

| Activation | Variable tracking | Std. dev. | Passkey | Multi-query | Wall time |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Squared ReLU | 89.58% | 12.98% | 64.58% | 15.76% | 123.67 s |
| Squared GELU | **93.36%** | **5.47%** | 51.95% | 12.24% | 127.27 s |
| Weighted schedule | 91.54% | 10.28% | 48.05% | 12.50% | 135.44 s |
| Stochastic hard schedule | 91.80% | 7.11% | 59.90% | 13.41% | 128.65 s |

## Findings

1. GELU² is the strongest and most stable in-domain activation in this test.
2. ReLU² transfers better to the two untrained tasks, but has much higher
   seed variance.
3. The weighted schedule computes both branches, is slowest, and does not
   combine their advantages. It should not be the default.
4. The stochastic schedule improves stability over ReLU² and transfers better
   than GELU² while costing roughly the same as GELU².
5. A three-repeat throughput-only benchmark (300 training steps, one
   evaluation batch) measured GELU² at `29.25 ± 0.68 s` and the stochastic
   schedule at `29.97 ± 0.89 s`. The apparent schedule overhead is 2.44%, but
   the distributions overlap and the mean difference is only about 1.1 pooled
   standard errors. This does not establish a statistically reliable slowdown.

## Engineering findings

- `torch.lerp` with a BF16 tensor weight failed under the CUDA autocast path;
  multiplicative interpolation fixed the weighted implementation.
- Sampling inside every activation forward introduces avoidable Python and
  synchronization overhead. Sampling once per optimizer step and hot-swapping
  native activation modules removes it from the GPU-critical path.

## Next tests

- repeat throughput with interleaved run order, warm-up exclusion, and more
  repetitions if a sub-3% runtime claim matters;
- compare delayed, cosine, and block-wise stochastic schedules;
- repeat on longer contexts and a larger trained task;
- report convergence curves, peak memory, power, and energy rather than final
  accuracy alone;
- test whether ReLU² transfer gains persist at matched in-domain accuracy.
